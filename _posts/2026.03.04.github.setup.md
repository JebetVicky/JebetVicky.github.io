## Why I Finally Set Up My GitHub

Today I set up my GitHub properly.

Not just as a code repository but as a space to document growth.

For a long time, I’ve worked behind the scenes: cleaning datasets, digitizing maps, validating spatial layers, building dashboards, supporting Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL) frameworks. Important work. Technical work. Impact-driven work.

But invisible work.

This GitHub is my decision to make the learning visible.

---

## Where I’m Coming From

I work at the intersection of:

- GIS & Remote Sensing  
- Climate resilience and regenerative land systems  
- Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL)  
- Data cleaning and validation for responsible AI  

Most real world datasets are messy.  
Most environmental systems are complex.  
Most impact stories are incomplete without good data.

I’ve learned that before AI, before dashboards, before insights there must be structure.

There must be clean data.

There must be intention.

---

## What I Hope This Becomes

I hope this space becomes:

- A living portfolio of projects and experiments  
- A record of lessons (including mistakes)  
- A place where I connect GIS, AI, and environmental systems  
- Proof that growth happens through practice  

Not everything here will be perfect.

But it will be honest.

---

## The Bigger Vision

I believe geospatial data can help us:

- Understand land use change  
- Improve food systems  
- Measure climate resilience  
- Support better policy decisions  

And I believe Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL) is how we ensure that impact is real not just reported.

This GitHub is a small but intentional step toward that bigger vision.
